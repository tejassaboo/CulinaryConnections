According to a study from Cornell’s School of Applied Economics and Management, firefighters who eat together perform better in the line
of duty.

Though we may not all be in life-or-death situations, the power of food to unite and bridge gaps is undeniable.

Impacts

Increased Workplace Happiness: Eating together is a form of a networking activity that can contribute to an employee's sense of belonging in the overarching company family.

Increased Employee Retention: In an informal experiment conducted by a Californian startup, the tradition of eating lunch together on Fridays led to a higher percentage of employees staying with the company.

Increased Productivity: The bane of every employee--time spent working inefficiently and on the wrong priorities. A healthy diet can lend itself to the focus necessary to tackle tough tasks.
